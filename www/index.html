About the Project

This is a Python script that randomly generates songs in the LilyPond markup language. LilyPond is a simple LaTeX-based markup language that allows for the creation of beautiful sheet music and sonorous MIDI output.

The script uses a variety of stochastic algorithms to generate both consistently consonant sounds as well as variance in the output. Some of the algorithms we are currently using include third-order Markov chains for chord progressions and simple random choices for rhythms and instrument choices. In the future, we will implement cellular automata and genetic (chromosome-based) algorithms to facilitate the "organized" randomness of the songs.

Among the features that are common in each piece of music as of now are:

  • High drum section (timpanis, cymbals, etc.)
  • Low drum section (snare drums, among others)
  • Electric piano chord section
  • Melody section

The various randomized parameters include:

  • Tempo
  • Chord progression (based on a third-order Markov chain kindly borrowed from HookTheory)
  • Phrase length for drums
  • Chord length for piano
  • Melodies, also based on a Markov chain trained using the Nottingham Folk Music Database

About Us

Juan Guzman

Juan started with no knowledge of programming and left with an infinite increase in programming knowhow. He provided many ideas on how to improve the song outputs of the script. He learned that while entertaining and interesting, programming was not for him, though he is glad to have done programming for the experience it offered him.

Spencer Weiss

Spencer did most of the coding for the project, writing the script to generate the songs and the website to present the information and the favorites of the output of our work. Of the programming languages used in this project, the one Spencer enjoyed learning the most about was Python, as he had very little experience with it beforehand. This project reinvigorated Spencer's interest in studying computer science in college.

Brian Ritchken

Brian provided many of the initial rhythms used in the drum sections of the songs. Living a childhood consisting primarily of music, Brian was able to truly inspire the rest of us. As a result of hearing Dancer in the Morn’, Brian then decided to live a life of celibacy—for he realized the only way to achieve true euphoria and ultimate nirvana in his life was to continue listening to songs produced by MusicGen.
